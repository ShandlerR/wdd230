Founding of Carlsbad

Carlsbad, California, originally known as Frazier's Station, traces its roots back to the late 19th century when a well driller named John Frazier tapped into a mineral spring in the area. The discovery of these healing waters led to the establishment of a health spa and resort destination. Renamed Carlsbad in the 1880s, the town rapidly grew as visitors flocked to experience the therapeutic benefits of its mineral springs.

Transformation into a Coastal Gem

Throughout the early 20th century, Carlsbad evolved from a small agricultural community into a vibrant coastal city. The arrival of the Santa Fe Railway in 1887 facilitated transportation and brought in tourists and settlers. The area's fertile soil supported the growth of flower fields, earning Carlsbad the nickname "The Flower Capital of the World." Today, Carlsbad retains its charm with a blend of rich history, stunning beaches, and a thriving tourist industry.
